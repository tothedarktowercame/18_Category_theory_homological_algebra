\subsection*{Synopsis}
Abell√°n, Haugseng, and Martini give a fibration-centric toolkit for $(\infty,2)$-categories. They characterize fibrations by pullback squares, construct free fibrations admitting prescribed (co)cartesian lifts, and describe adjoints to restriction along partial fibrations. These ingredients feed into lax/oplax (co)limit constructions and Kan extensions in a model-independent fashion, yielding cofinality statements and presentations of presentable $(\infty,2)$-categories as accessible localizations.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Characterizes fibrations of $(\infty,2)$-categories via a universal pullback property.
  \item Builds free fibrations with constrained lift data and identifies adjoints to restriction functors.
  \item Develops fibrational descriptions of lax, oplax, and weighted (co)limits plus partially lax Kan extensions.
  \item Applies the machinery to cofinality and presentability results for $(\infty,2)$-categories.
\end{enumerate}
