\subsection*{Synopsis}
Ghojogh and Amanpour propose similarity algebra, a framework for algebraic/Lie structures satisfying axioms up to a tolerance $\varepsilon$. They develop similarity groups, rings, etc., show collapse to classical structures under $\varepsilon \to 0$, define morphisms, and compare with fuzzy algebra.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item defines similarity algebraic structures with quantitative bounds;
  \item establishes collapse theorems under uniform control;
  \item introduces similarity categories and morphisms; and
  \item relates similarity algebra to fuzzy algebra.
\end{enumerate}
