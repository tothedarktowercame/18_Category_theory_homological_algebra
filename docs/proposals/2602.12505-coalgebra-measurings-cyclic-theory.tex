\subsection*{Synopsis}
Banerjee and Kour study induced maps between Hochschild/cyclic homology arising from coalgebra measurings. They prove compatibility with $\lambda$-decompositions, products, and module structures, then compare with homologies of matrix algebras across cyclic, Leibniz, and dihedral contexts via primitive parts of Lie/Leibniz homology.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Defines coalgebra measurings and induced maps on Hochschild/cyclic homology.
  \item Shows these maps respect $\lambda$-decomposition, products, and module structures.
  \item Relates induced maps to those on matrix algebra homologies (cyclic, Lie, Leibniz, dihedral).
\end{enumerate}
