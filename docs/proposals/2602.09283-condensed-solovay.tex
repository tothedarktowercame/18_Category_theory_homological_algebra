\subsection*{Synopsis}
Bannister and Basak construct a geometric morphism from the Grothendieck topos of the Solovay model to the $κ$-pyknotic sets of Barwick-Haine and Clausen-Scholze. Using this and automatic continuity they reprove Clausen-Scholze's Whitehead problem solution for condensed abelian groups and compute internal $Ext$ for locally compact groups.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item builds the geometric morphism from the Solovay topos to $κ$-pyknotic sets;
  \item applies automatic continuity to condensed abelian groups;
  \item recovers the Whitehead problem solution; and
  \item computes internal $Ext$ for locally compact abelian groups.
\end{enumerate}
