\subsection*{Synopsis}
Exit weaving enriches the exit-path $\infty$-groupoid of a stratified space by decorating every stratum with a span-valued transport rule. The authors:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item build an explicit \emph{weave nerve} $\mathcal{W}(X)$ from iterated spans in $\mathrm{Span}_2(\mathrm{Exit}(X))$;
  \item construct factorization charts on constructible stacks by gluing the local weaves along tangential smoothings;
  \item prove that perverse schobers are classified by locally constant modules over $\mathcal{W}(X)$; and
  \item supply detection criteria (using exit-path monodromy) that decide when a weave is trivial.
\end{enumerate}
